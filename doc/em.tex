\section{Conventions}



\subsection{UDCA: Upside-Down Count and Attitude}

``A low card in a suit encourages, a high card discourages.''
Attitude: Playing to a partner's lead, a partner's void, or discarding to the declarer signals the strength of that suit.  High card shows weakness, low card shows strength.
Count: Playing to the declarer's lead signals the length of that suit.  High card shows an odd number, low card shows an even number.

\subsection{Odd-Even Discard}

``Like odd discards, show direction on even discards.''
When first making a discard, discarding an odd-numbered card shows preference for the suit.  An even-numbered card shows preference for one of the other two suits.  A low even discard prefers the lower suit, a high even discard prefers the higher unplayed suit.
